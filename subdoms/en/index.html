Accommodation in the Table Mountains, Broumovsko region, Machovská Lhota. A sunny place in an attractive, undiscovered tourist area between the Broumov Walls and the Polish Table Mountains near Adršpach. Quiet rural atmosphere. Stylish environment in an old guesthouse from 1884.
Our pension is located in the protected landscape area of Broumov in the romantic village of Machovská Lhota, which is surrounded on one side by the National Park Table Mountains with the famous Velká Hejšovina and on the other side by the Broumov Walls, the popular Adršpach is 22 km away from us. Machov region is a paradise for hiking, cycling, cross-country skiing and bouldering. Lovers of a quiet rural atmosphere and family environment will find their place here.

The story of the pension

Šrám family – 1884 – 1950
The original owner was Mr. Eduard Šrám. He added a pub and also a guest room on the 1 st floor to the original Broumov-type farmhouse. He lived in the guesthouse together with his wife and eight children. He also created and expanded the social hall for the needs of the Lhota Volunteer Theatre. He was additionally an innkeeper who, after the declaration of war in 1914, allegedly hit a picture of the emperor with a pint in the pub out of anger and broke it, as can be seen on it to this day.
Lidman family - 1950-2014
In 1950, the Lidman family bought the guesthouse, but in 1953 the communist regime nationalised it. At least they could live and work there. The Lidman pub was frequented by groups of tramps until the late 1980s because here, "at the end of the world,"" near the hermetically sealed border, they remained outside the regime´s reach. In the 1990s, the Lidman family got the guest house back and ran it until 2014.
Šturm family since 2014
Since 2014 it has been owned by the Šturm family.
